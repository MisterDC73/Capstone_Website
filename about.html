About Our Project

Understanding Wall-ECE: Design, Competition, and Innovaiton

What is Wall-ECE?

Wall-ECE is a fully autonomous disaster response robot designed to aid first responders in collecting and managing debris at disaster sites. The system demonstrates advanced capabilities in object detection, autonomous material handling, sorting, and placement—all while operating within strict physical constraints.

The Big Idea

Disasters cost communities over $300 billion USD annually. First responders need intelligent systems to quickly manage debris and supplies. Wall-ECE proves that autonomous robotics can handle this challenge, enabling faster response times and reducing the physical burden on emergency personnel.

The robot successfully identifies four types of colored chips, retrieves rubber ducks, and stacks colored pedestals—all in a single autonomous mission. This proof-of-concept technology is expandable to real-world applications in municipal waste management, nuclear site cleanup, and hazardous material handling.

The ECE Hardware Competition

Wall-ECE was developed for the ECE Hardware Competition at Mississippi State University, a prestigious event that challenges students to design and build innovative embedded systems.

🚨 The Size Constraint Challenge

Perhaps the most significant engineering challenge: Wall-ECE must fit within a 12-inch cube and weigh less than 25 pounds. This constraint shaped every design decision and forced innovative engineering solutions.

Why Size Matters

Operating in disaster zones means navigating tight, confined spaces. Emergency response robots must be compact enough to fit through doorways, climb rubble, and maneuver in cluttered environments—all while carrying heavy loads of debris and maintaining stability.

Size Limit

Wall-ECE must fit within a cubic boundary of 12 inches on each side.

Maximum Dimensions
12" × 12" × 12"

Weight Limit

Lighter weight improves portability and enables faster and more accurate movement.

Maximum Weight
25 lbs

The Engineering Struggle

The Challenge: Fitting a robot with 5 independent subsystems, multiple motors, servo drivers, sensors, and a microcontroller into a 12" cube was like solving a 3D puzzle. Every component had to be carefully positioned, and wiring had to be routed with surgical precision.

The Solution: Our team:

  • ✓ Used PCB to maximize space in the robot
  • ✓ Custom-designed mechanical mounts to reduce wasted space and weight
  • ✓ Selected compact, high-efficiency motors and sensors
  • ✓ Implemented lightweight composite materials for structure
  • ✓ Optimized component placement through 3D CAD design

Design Requirements

Functional Requirements

  • Sort chips by color (Red, Green, Yellow, Blue)
  • Collect rubber ducks to designated zone
  • Stack colored pedestals in correct order
  • Transport items to respective locations
  • Run three 3-minute rounds consecutively without recharge

Performance Targets

  • 80%+ read accuracy on color detection
  • 2" precision wall navigation (tight spaces)
  • Rapid item pickup (<2 seconds per item)
  • Reliable autonomous operation (no manual intervention)
  • Modular design for quick repairs between rounds
