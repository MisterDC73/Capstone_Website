Meet Scrap Squad

The Team Behind Wall-ECE

Scrap Squad is a dedicated group of Electrical and Computer Engineering students at Mississippi State University who combined their expertise in embedded systems, mechanical design, and software development to create Wall-ECE. Each team member brought unique skills and perspectives to solve complex engineering challenges.

Oshiv Yuvaraj
Team Lead • Duck Wrangling
Electrical Engineering
Oshiv led the team through the project lifecycle, coordinating between subsystems and ensuring smooth integration. His focus was on the duck wrangling system—designing the mechanism to reliably capture and transport rubber ducks.
🏅 Passionate about robotics competitions and autonomous systems
Dexter Fason
Project Manager • Locomotion
Computer Engineering
As PM, Dexter managed schedules and deliverables while spearheading the locomotion subsystem. He designed Wall-ECE's omnidirectional movement using Mecanum wheels, solving the tight-space navigation challenges.
⚡ Expert in motor control and dynamic systems
Cade Teeple
Chip Handling Specialist
Electrical Engineering
Cade engineered the chip handling subsystem, implementing the servo-driven sorting mechanism that separates colored chips with 90%+ accuracy. His mechanical designs were crucial to maximizing space efficiency.
🎯 Master of precision mechanical design in constrained spaces
Millicent Steadman
Sensing Systems Engineer
Computer Engineering
Millicent developed the sensing subsystem, integrating color sensors, time-of-flight distance detection, and gyroscopes. Her work on sensor calibration and data fusion was critical to Wall-ECE's autonomous perception.
🔍 Specializes in sensor fusion and embedded signal processing
Seth Aultman
Pedestal Stacking Engineer
Electrical Engineering
Seth designed and implemented the pedestal stacking subsystem, which identifies colored pedestals and places them in the correct order. His work on servo control and placement accuracy was essential to competition success.
🏗️ Expert in precision positioning and mechanical timing

Faculty Advisor

Benjamin Wilkinson

A PHD student of the Electrical and Computer Engineering at Mississippi State University. Future Doctor Benjamin Wilkinson provided technical guidance, mentorship, and invaluable expertise throughout the Wall-ECE project. His background in embedded systems and robotics helped the team navigate complex engineering challenges.
